Discover Interesting Places

Utah's Hogle Zoo

Utah's Hogle Zoo is located at 2600 E Sunnyside Ave, Salt Lake City, UT 84108, a member of AZA, known for connecting people with wildlife through exhibits like African Savanna, Rocky Shores, and Asian Highlands, aiming to inspire conservation with its mission to create wildlife champions, offering family fun and educational experiences.

Natural History Museum of Utah

Natural History Museum of Utah (NHMU) Address: 301 Wakara Way, Salt Lake City, UT 84108 (Rio Tinto Center). Explores Utah's natural world with vast collections, featuring dinosaur exhibits, Native American cultures (Native Voices), ecosystems, and life sciences in an award-winning building on the University of Utah campus.

Clark Planetarium

The Clark Planetarium is a downtown Salt Lake City science museum at 110 S 400 W, Salt Lake City, UT 84101, offering free, hands-on space/science exhibits and paid shows in its dome and IMAX theaters, aiming to inspire curiosity about the universe through interactive learning for all ages.

Timpanogos Cave

Timpanogos Cave is part of the Timpanogos Cave National Monument, located at 2038 W. Alpine Loop Road, American Fork, UT 84003, within the Wasatch Mountains, known for its stunning, colorful formations like helictites, accessed via a strenuous but paved 1.5-mile hike from the visitor center for guided tours from spring to fall.

Park City Resorts

Park City, Utah boasts major ski resorts like Park City Mountain Resort (7300 acres, diverse terrain, base at 1345 Lowell Ave) and Deer Valley Resort (luxury focus, near Empire/Lady Morgan lifts), plus others like Montage Deer Valley (luxury) and Woodward Park City (action sports). These resorts offer year-round activities, from world-class skiing/snowboarding (groomed runs, bowls, parks) to summer mountain biking and hiking, all accessible from SLC Airport, making it a premier destination.

Utah State Capitol

The Utah State Capitol, located at 350 North State Street, Salt Lake City, UT 84114, is a grand neoclassical building with a prominent dome, housing the state's legislature, governor, and courts, featuring Utah granite, a grand rotunda with murals, and beautiful grounds with monuments, serving as a historic landmark and seat of government since its 1916 opening.

City Creek Center

City Creek Center a major downtown Salt Lake City shopping mall at 50 S Main St, SLC, UT 84101, known for its retractable roof, flowing creek with fish, and mix of retail (Nordstrom, Macy's, Tiffany, etc.) and dining, creating a unique, open-air (or enclosed) urban shopping experience.

Antelope Island

Antelope Island State Park, located near Syracuse, Utah, is the largest island in the Great Salt Lake, famous for its free-roaming bison, pronghorn, deer, and bighorn sheep, offering activities like hiking, biking, camping, historic ranch visits (Fielding Garr Ranch), birding, and incredible sunsets, accessible via a causeway from the mainland and featuring facilities for visitors. With address 4528 W. 1700 S., Syracuse, UT 84075.
